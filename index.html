About Me

My name is Marcus "Bitsauce" Loo Vergara, and I am a computer science nerd. I am currently attening my last semester of a 6 year long MSc degree in computer science from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology.

Already in elementary school, I had a facination for all things computer and technology. Growing up in the era when 3D graphics just hit the home consoles, I gained initial interest in learning about these technologies. Determined to achieve my goal, I started to learn how to program at the age of 13 with the help of Torque game eninge.

Games & Graphics

My journey in programming started by the desire to create experiences that I would enjoy myself, and that I could share with other people. Along the way I got an interest for the more technological side of games, in particular real-time 3D graphics. Struck with a desire to understand graphics on a more fundamental level, I sought to create a simple game engine with C++ and OpenGL. The ultimate goal would be to create a 2D game with this engine.

Overworld

Overworld is the most comprehensive of these projects – an infinite, tile-based sandbox akin to Terraria and Minecraft. The project has been in development since November 2011, however its development has been put on ice due to focus on the Master's thesis. I have learned a lot about optimization for games, and how to ensure efficient communication between the GPU and CPU through the project. Maintaining a stable frame rate when generation has to happen on the fly is difficult, and lead me to utilize the GPU for general purpose computing to ease the load on the CPU.

Physically Based Shading

During the summer of 2018 I got an amazing opportunity – to join PlayStation's Global Developer Technology Group, and work on various projects related to PS4 graphics. One of these projects pertained to the implementation of Physically Based Shading. I learned a lot about topics in advanced 3D graphics: HDR lighting, the "metallic workflow," GPU architecture, etc.

Machine Learning & Master's Thesis

There has been a lot of interest in artificial intelligence as of late, and I view these technologies with curiousity and fascination. These technologies have potential. My degree is in visual compution, where computer vision held a significant role. As such, I have taken several courses and done multiple replication studies as class projects. As with graphics, I wanted to understand deep learning on the most fundamental level, and therefore opt to implement papers from scratch, and with modifications that I deem worthy of experimentation.

For my thesis, I ended up delving into the topic of deep reinforcement learning for self-driving vehicles. So far, I implemented the state-of-the-art method in reinforcement learning, known as Proximal Policy Optimization.
